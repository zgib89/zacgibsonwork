Ordinary surfaces, catastrophic meaning

The lie is not a twist. It is a system.

Silas Westfall is trained to survive pressure, read rooms, and trust a chain of command that sounds legitimate until ordinary proof starts contradicting it.

The story does not ask the reader to believe in a conspiracy because someone says the word. It builds the case through a public computer, a roster, a cache, a witness, and records that make the official version impossible.

The orders were the trap.
